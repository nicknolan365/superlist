Why, Arizona exists because the state requires town names to have at least three letters. The intersection of Routes 85 and 86 formed a "Y," but a single letter wouldn't do. So they named it "Why." Population: about 116. The most frequently asked question when you tell someone where you live is, appropriately, "why?"
