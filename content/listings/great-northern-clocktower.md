The Great Northern Railway Depot Tower in Spokane, Washington, is a physical reminder of when railroads made Spokane a regional distribution hub in the late 1800s and early 1900s. The clocktower is one of Spokane's most recognizable landmarks and has outlasted the railroad it was built to serve. It just stands there, telling time, being handsome, and reminding people that trains used to matter more.
